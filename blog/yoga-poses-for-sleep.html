5 Yoga Poses That Prepare Your Body for Deep Sleep

By University of Today  ·  5 min read  ·  Sleep Health

Most people think of yoga as an energising practice. But a specific set of poses — held gently, at low intensity, in the right sequence — has a profoundly sedating effect on the nervous system. These are not power yoga poses. They are slow, restorative postures that physically prepare your body for deep sleep.

Why Yoga Helps with Sleep

During the day, stress causes us to hold tension in our muscles — particularly in the hips, lower back, shoulders and jaw. This physical tension persists when we lie down at night, keeping the nervous system alert even when we are exhausted.

Gentle yoga before bed releases this stored muscular tension, lowers cortisol, and activates the parasympathetic nervous system. Several studies have found that a regular pre-sleep yoga practice reduces the time it takes to fall asleep by an average of 15–20 minutes.

5 Yoga Poses for Deep Sleep

1. Legs Up the Wall (Viparita Karani)

Lie on your back and extend your legs vertically up a wall. Stay for 5–10 minutes. This simple inversion reverses blood flow from the legs back to the core, calms the nervous system, and reduces cortisol rapidly. It is often called the "most relaxing pose in all of yoga" — and it requires zero flexibility.

2. Child's Pose (Balasana)

Kneel on your mat, sit back on your heels, and extend your arms forward as you lower your forehead to the floor. Stay for 2–3 minutes. This pose decompresses the spine, calms the adrenal glands (which produce cortisol), and activates the body's rest response through forward folding.

3. Supine Spinal Twist (Supta Matsyendrasana)

Lie on your back, bring one knee to your chest, then guide it across your body to the opposite side. Extend your arm out to the side. Hold 2 minutes each side. Spinal twists wring tension from the back, stimulate the vagus nerve, and release held stress from the day.

The vagus nerve — the main nerve of the parasympathetic nervous system — runs close to the spine. Gentle twisting and forward-folding poses directly stimulate it, creating a rapid calming effect.

4. Seated Forward Fold (Paschimottanasana)

Sit with your legs extended. Inhale to lengthen your spine, then exhale and gently fold forward over your legs. Hold for 3 minutes. Forward folds are inherently calming — they send a "safety" signal to the nervous system and reduce cortisol more effectively than any other type of movement.

5. Corpse Pose (Savasana)

Lie flat on your back, arms slightly away from the body, palms facing up. Stay completely still for 5–10 minutes, doing slow belly breathing. This is the final pose in every yoga class for a reason — it integrates the benefits of all the other poses and brings the body to the threshold of sleep.

The Ideal Pre-Sleep Yoga Sequence

For best results, practise this sequence in the following order, starting 30–45 minutes before you want to be asleep:

  1. Child's Pose — 3 minutes
  2. Supine Spinal Twist — 2 minutes each side
  3. Seated Forward Fold — 3 minutes
  4. Legs Up the Wall — 8 minutes
  5. Savasana with breathwork — 5–10 minutes

Total time: approximately 25 minutes. Most people are asleep within minutes of getting into bed after this sequence.

Making It a Habit

The power of yoga for sleep builds over time. After 2–3 weeks of consistent practice, your body begins to recognise the poses as a sleep signal — and the transition from the mat to deep sleep becomes faster and more reliable with each passing night.
